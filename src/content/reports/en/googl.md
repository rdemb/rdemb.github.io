## Summary

High quality (ROE 27.6%, net margin 37.8%, operating 36.9%) comes with real growth: EPS +34.5%, net income +32%, revenue +13.9% year over year. At a PEG near 0.99 the 34 P/E is largely covered by the pace of EPS growth, so the stock is less stretched than the P/E alone suggests. The market still trades the narrative that "AI will kill search," and the numbers do not confirm it. The main risk sits in revenue concentration in Search ads and antitrust, not in the rate of growth. Verdict: watch closely, accumulate for those who judge the AI fear to be overdone.

## Business and Moat

ROE 27.6%, operating margin 36.9%, net 37.8%, revenue growth 13.9% with EPS growth of 34.5% (net income +32%). Distribution (Android, Chrome, YouTube), Cloud, in-house TPUs. The moat is genuine, though the core (Search ads, ~57% of revenue) remains under AI pressure. The balance sheet is conservative: D/E 17%, current ratio 1.92. The company carries net debt of roughly $41B (cash $38B, debt $79B), but leverage is low and poses no solvency threat.

## Valuation and Scenarios

- P/E 34.1, P/B 9.4, P/S 12.8, PEG ~0.99. With EPS growth of +34.5%, the valuation is mostly justified by growth rather than optimism alone. FCF yield 1.63% (free cash flow burdened by peak AI capex).
- Base case: if EPS growth holds around 20-30%, today's multiple is defensible. The main swing factor is not the valuation but the durability of Search monetization in the AI era.
- Graham (conservative, defensive): the Graham number is about $98, P/E×P/B = 320 against a 22.5 threshold. This method penalizes growth companies harshly and serves here as a lower anchor, not a target.

## Red Team Test (premortem)

A year out, −30 to −50%: antitrust forces a breakup (Search/AdTech) or AI permanently erodes Search ads (fewer paid clicks, lower CPC). The most fragile assumption is the durability of Search monetization in the AI era, not the growth rate itself, which is strong today. The premortem does not undermine quality or growth; it targets the concentration of a single revenue stream on the front line of disruption.

## Risk and Position Sizing

The balance sheet rules out ruin (D/E 17%, current 1.92). The real risk is revenue concentration in Search ads and the antitrust ruling, which in a bad scenario gives a permanent rerating of −30 to −50%. Moderate position, built gradually. Signals to track: the antitrust ruling, search market share, AI Overviews monetization, the AI capex trajectory.

## Committee Verdict

Watch closely, with a bias toward accumulation. Clean figures from the filings show a business less stretched than the P/E alone implies: EPS growth of +34.5% brings the PEG to about 0.99. Accumulate for those who judge the AI fear to be overdone, with full awareness of Search concentration and regulatory risk. A full position only once the moat and antitrust risks become clearer.
