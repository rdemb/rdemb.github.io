## Summary

Outstanding quality (ROE 39%, net margin 38%, net cash +$31B), earnings up 82%, while the market trades the narrative that "AI will kill search." Soros: this is a reflexive anti-bubble, a crowd error (the numbers rise, the narrative frightens). But the stock is near its high, and the entire thesis hangs on a single revenue stream (Search ads) threatened by AI and antitrust. Verdict: hold and watch, light accumulation only for those who believe the fear is overdone.

## Business and Moat

ROE 38.9%, gross margin 60%, operating 36%, net 38%, revenue growth 22% / earnings growth 82%. Distribution (Android, Chrome, YouTube), Cloud, TPU. The moat is genuine, but under AI pressure at its core (Search ads are ~57% of revenue).

## Valuation and Scenarios

- Intrinsic value (DCF + owner earnings): ~$250-330. The $368 price is above the upper bound. The margin is negative (−10 to −25%).
- Graham (conservatively): $160-200. Free cash flow is distorted by peak AI capex ($91B FY).

## Red Team Test (premortem)

A year out, −30 to −50%: antitrust forces a breakup (Search/AdTech) or AI permanently erodes search ads (fewer paid clicks, lower CPC). The most fragile assumption: the durability of the Search moat in the AI era. The thesis survived only as a "limited position," not as a strong buy.

## Risk and Position Sizing

The balance sheet rules out ruin, but concentration in a single revenue stream on the front line of disruption is a risk of a permanent rerating of −30 to −50%. Limited position. Signals to track: the antitrust ruling, search market share, AI Overviews monetization.

## Committee Verdict

Watch closely. Light accumulation for those who believe the Soros thesis (the fear is overdone); value waits at $270-290. Not a full position until the moat and regulatory risks become clearer.
