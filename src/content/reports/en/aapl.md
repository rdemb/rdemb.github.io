## Summary

Apple is an outstanding business with a genuine moat, but at ~$307 there is no margin of safety. The three core minds, each by their own path, reached a consistent conclusion: a gem, but too expensive, wait for a pullback.

## Council Consensus

Apple is a cash-generating machine. From the SEC filings: ROE 105%, gross margin 46.9%, operating margin 32%, net margin 26.9%, nearly $99B of free cash flow per year. The debate is not about quality, only about price. The valuation is stretched: P/E 41.2 on EPS of 7.46, PEG 1.81, P/B 42.4, FCF yield 2.19%, dividend 0.34%. The $4.5T market capitalization exceeds even a bullish DCF ($2.8T).

## Council Vote

| Graham | bearish · 0.85 | P/E×P/B 1747 vs threshold 22.5 (78× too expensive) |
| Lynch | neutral · 0.60 | PEG 1.81; "stalwart"; waits for P/E ~28 |

## Valuation and Entry Thresholds

- **Buffett**: margin of safety at ~$150-185 (P/FCF 25-30x).
- **Graham**: strictly defensive, prudent value ~$75-112.
- **Lynch**: at a P/E of ~28, around ~$230.

Council threshold: consider buying only on a clear pullback, when the P/E falls toward 25-28 (roughly $200-230 or lower).

## Principal Risk

Multiple compression from P/E 41.2 toward 25-28 means a permanent loss even with solid results. Revenue grows only 6.4% per year, though net income rises 19.5% and EPS 22.7% on the back of buybacks. The liquidity position is thin (current ratio 1.07) and debt to equity reaches 78%.

## Committee Verdict

Keep Apple on the watchlist as a gem. Do not add at $307. Consider buying on a pullback toward $200-230, aggressively closer to $150-185. If you already hold a position: hold the quality, do not sell in panic.
