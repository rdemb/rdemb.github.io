## Summary

Apple is an outstanding business with a genuine moat, but at ~$307 there is no margin of safety. The three core minds, each by their own path, reached a consistent conclusion: a gem, but too expensive, wait for a pullback.

## Council Consensus

Apple is a cash-generating machine: ROE 141%, operating margin 32%, gross margin 48%, ~$101B of free cash flow per year. The debate is not about quality, only about price. The valuation is stretched: P/E 37 (fwd 32), PEG ~1.5-1.7, P/B 42, EV/EBITDA 28. The $4.5T market capitalization exceeds even a bullish DCF ($2.8T).

## Council Vote

| mind | signal | key point |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Buffett | neutral · 0.80 | DCF $1.6-2.8T vs market cap $4.5T; entry $150-185 |
| Graham | bearish · 0.85 | P/E×P/B = 1573 vs threshold 22.5 (70× too expensive) |
| Lynch | neutral · 0.60 | PEG 1.47-1.71; "stalwart"; waits for P/E ~28 |

## Valuation and Entry Thresholds

- **Buffett**: margin of safety at ~$150-185 (P/FCF 25-30x).
- **Graham**: strictly defensive, prudent value ~$75-112.
- **Lynch**: at a P/E of ~28 and PEG ~1, around ~$230.

Council threshold: consider buying only on a clear pullback, when the P/E falls toward 25-28 (roughly $200-230 or lower).

## Principal Risk

Multiple compression (P/E 37 to 25-28 means a permanent loss even with solid results), slowing growth (quarterly earnings 19% vs annual 22%), a thin liquidity position (current ratio 1.07).

## Committee Verdict

Keep Apple on the watchlist as a gem. Do not add at $307. Consider buying on a pullback toward $200-230, aggressively closer to $150-185. If you already hold a position: hold the quality, do not sell in panic.
