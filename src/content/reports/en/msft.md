## Summary

A top-quality company with a fortress balance sheet. The 25% decline reflects the market cooling on artificial-intelligence spending, while the business itself remains strong. Soros sees an anti-bubble here, because the price fell faster than analyst expectations. Graham soberly warns that, in absolute terms, the company is still expensive. The council's verdict: this is quality worth owning, best accumulated patiently and in small tranches.

## Business and Moat

A durable advantage visible in the numbers: ROE 34%, gross margin 68%, operating 46%, net 39%, revenue growth 18% and earnings growth 23%. Azure, Microsoft 365, and high switching costs build a genuine moat grounded in years of advantage.

## Valuation and Scenarios

DCF on free cash flow, discount rate 9-10%:

- **Bear** (FCF declines on capex): ~$180
- **Base**: ~$261
- **Bull** (15%→7%): ~$346

The $417 price sits above the upper bound of reasonable value. The margin of safety is negative under every assumption. The key catch: real FCF fell to ~$37B (from $72B FY), because AI capex grew to ~$65B per year.

## Red Team Test (premortem)

A year out, −30%: AI capex disappoints (weak data-center returns) and multiple compression (forward P/E 21→16, around $320) hit at once. The most fragile assumption: AI monetization (Copilot, Azure-AI). The thesis survived as "accumulate," not as "buy aggressively."

## Risk and Position Sizing

89% of the valuation is expected growth (a valuation tail, not a solvency tail). A full position is permissible from a ruin perspective, but scale it against the valuation and enter on a staggered basis over time. The signal to track: the free-cash-flow trend (whether capex begins converting into cash).

## Committee Verdict

Accumulate quality in tranches on weakness. At $417, cautiously and in small size; add at $350 and below; full margin at $250-300. Do not sell quality in panic.
