Your future customer increasingly does not type the question into Google. They ask ChatGPT or Perplexity: "which company for [X] would you recommend in [my city]?" And they get a ready answer with a few names. There is one question: is yours among them?

You can check this yourself, in five minutes, for free.

## Run the test

Open ChatGPT and type exactly what your customer would type. Not your company name, the problem: "recommend [your service] in [your city]" or "best [your trade] for [type of customer]". Ask a few variants. See whether your company appears in the answer.

If you do not appear, you are not alone. Most small companies are invisible to AI.

## Why this happens

AI does not check any live database of companies. It reconstructs the answer from what it has seen many times in credible places online. If there are no mentions of your company anywhere, the model simply does not know it exists. And if it does not know, it will not recommend. This is not a punishment for a bad site, it is the absence of a trace.

## What to do about it, honestly

Three things genuinely raise the chance that AI starts to include you:

- **Let yourself be read.** Your site must clearly say what you do and for whom, in plain text, not hidden behind scripts. AI crawlers have to be able to read it.
- **Answer your customer's questions** in your content. What you explain to clients on the phone, write it on the site. The model likes concrete answers.
- **Exist beyond your own site.** Most AI recommendations come from external sources. A mention in an industry article weighs more than a sentence on your own page.

## The limit nobody mentions

Nobody can guarantee you a spot in ChatGPT's answer. You can only raise the odds and measure the effect before and after. The first changes usually show after two to four months. If someone promises you the top spot in a week, they are selling you a fairy tale.
