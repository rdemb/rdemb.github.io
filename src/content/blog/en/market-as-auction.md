AAI — Adaptive Auction Intelligence — is how I organize the market before I make a decision. I treat the market as an auction, not as a panel of individual indicators.

The order is deliberate: market structure first, scenario second. Execution cost, data freshness and the memory of similar situations first — direction only after that. It's a layer meant to help the operator separate four things that are easy to confuse: context from setup, setup from execution, narrative from proof, activity from edge.

The key assumption: no edge is not a system failure. It's a result. A system that can say "there's nothing here" is worth more than one that always finds a reason to enter.

**What works:** AAI is local and deterministic — the same data gives the same answer, no black box.

**Limitation:** it's a layer that organizes a decision, not a money machine. The human at the desk and hard verification on data still matter.
