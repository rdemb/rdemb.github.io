I trade the market and I build software. It sounds like two different lives, but they taught me exactly the same thing.

The market is brutally honest. It does not care how elegant your idea is. Either you make money after costs or you do not. The same rule governs good code: it does not matter how nice it looks, it matters whether it works when real people use it.

## The simplest thing that could work, first

In trading I learned to check a dumb, simple reference point before I believe my own clever idea. In code it is the same: the simplest solution that works almost always beats the clever one you have to decode later.

## Test on what you have not seen

A strategy that only works on the past is worthless. Code that only works on my machine is too. What counts is how it behaves in conditions you did not foresee.

## Honesty about the result

This is the hardest and the most important. The market taught me to write down the failures too, because pretending a loss is "almost a profit" costs real money. Code is similar: a bug you hide from yourself comes back twice as angry.

## Why this matters to you

Because if you build anything, the same choice waits for you: a nice story or the truth. The market taught me to choose the truth, because you pay for the other option out of your own pocket.
