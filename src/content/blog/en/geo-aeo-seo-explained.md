In the last year SEO got two new acronyms: GEO and AEO. It sounds like another trend you have to pay extra for. Here is a plain take on what they are and what a small business really needs from them.

## Three acronyms, one question

- **SEO** is ranking high in Google when someone types a query. You have known this for years.
- **AEO** (Answer Engine Optimization) is being in the answer itself, not on a list of links. Google increasingly shows a ready answer at the top instead of ten blue links.
- **GEO** (Generative Engine Optimization) is being recommended by AI: ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini. A customer asks the AI, the AI names a few companies. You want to be one of them.

These are not three separate worlds. It is one question asked three ways: when someone looks for what you do, do they find you.

## What changed

Part of the traffic that used to go from Google to your site now ends inside an AI answer, with no click. If you exist only as one of ten links, you lose where the decision increasingly happens: in the ready answer.

## What a small business really needs

Honestly: do not buy three separate packages from three agencies. The foundation is shared and surprisingly small:

- a site a machine can read, plain text, not hidden behind scripts,
- content that answers your customer's real questions,
- a trace beyond your own site, because that is where AI takes most of its recommendations from.

Do these three things properly once, and they work for SEO, AEO and GEO at the same time. The rest is detail you add later.

## The honest catch

Nobody can guarantee you the top spot, in Google or in ChatGPT. You can only raise the odds and measure the effect before and after. The first changes show after two to four months. Anyone selling you "guaranteed AI rankings" is selling a fairy tale.
