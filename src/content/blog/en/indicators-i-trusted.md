Everyone starts the same way. RSI below 30, so I buy. Price touched the moving average, so it will bounce. MACD crossed, so we have a trend. It sounds like a system. For a long time I believed it.

Then I did something few people do: instead of believing, I tested. I took those indicators and checked them rigorously on historical data. With the real cost of every trade, and on data I had not seen before.

## What the data showed

Most of those "signals" are not signals. They are patterns our brain draws onto noise. On their own, after costs, classic indicators computed from price alone gave no edge in my tests. Some even lost. It is not a conspiracy, it is statistics: if everyone can see it, it is already in the price.

## That does not mean the chart is useless

It means an indicator is not an oracle, just a shortcut. Sometimes it helps as context, never as an order. The real edge, if it exists at all, lies elsewhere: in things that are harder to see and harder to measure.

## The takeaway

I did not throw the indicators in the bin. I threw out the belief that they know anything. It was an expensive lesson, but the most important one: before you believe something works, test it on data you do not know yet.
