Someone might ask what a toy like eight chess minds is doing in a studio that normally works with market data and automation. The answer is simple. It is the same workshop.

GAMBIT and forex-lab look completely different, but underneath they share the same thing: an engine computing live, an honest evaluation, and a decision you can trace. The difference is that with chess I can allow some lightness. No one loses money when a mind plays a sacrifice that does not pay off.

That makes play a good testing ground. Things that are stressful with real money are simply interesting in a game. And the habits stay. The same rigor that makes me count costs in forex-lab makes me show real ELO in GAMBIT instead of inflated numbers.

I build for joy and I measure with rigor. The two do not exclude each other, and the best projects often appear exactly where one meets the other.
