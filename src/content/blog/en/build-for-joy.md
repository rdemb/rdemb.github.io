Someone might ask what a toy like eight chess minds is doing in a studio that normally deals with market data and automation. The answer is simple: it's the same workshop.

GAMBIT and forex-lab look completely different, but underneath they share the same thing: an engine computing live, an honest evaluation function, and a decision you can trace. The difference is that with chess I can allow some lightness — no one loses money when a mind plays a sacrifice that doesn't pay off.

That makes play a good testing ground. Things that are stressful with real money are simply interesting in a game. And the habits stay: the same rigor that makes me count costs in forex-lab makes me show real ELO in GAMBIT, not inflated numbers.

I build for joy. I measure with rigor. The two don't exclude each other at all — and I suspect the best projects are born exactly where they meet.
