I have seen dozens of beautiful websites that bring in zero customers. Good looks are not the same as sales. Here is the difference.

## A pretty site pleases you. A selling site helps the customer.

In five seconds a customer wants to know three things: what you offer, whether it is for them, and what to do now. If they have to guess, they leave, even if the site looks like it belongs in a gallery.

## What actually makes a site sell

- **A clear offer.** One sentence: what you do and for whom. No jargon.
- **One call to action.** Not five links, one obvious next step.
- **Proof.** A real example, a review, a number. Something that says "this works".
- **Speed and readability.** For a human on a phone, and for the bot deciding whether to show you at all.

## The trap that is easy to fall into

The more effects, animation and "wow", the easier it is to lose the one thing: why the customer came. Decoration should support the message, not drown it. I say this as someone who likes pretty sites and builds them.

## How to check your own

Open your own site through the eyes of a stranger. In five seconds, do you know what to buy and why? If not, you have a pretty problem.

This is one of the dimensions I check in [a company X-ray](/preswietlenie/): not only whether you are visible, but whether the site turns visits into customers. Because traffic without conversion is just nice numbers.
