The currency market is entering the densest week of the quarter. In the background, oil at ninety-something dollars and inflation that came back even though it was supposed not to.

After the escalation between the US and Iran, oil is holding in a 90 to 97 dollar band. May CPI from the US came in at 4.2% year on year, the highest in three years. Central banks that were talking about cuts a quarter ago are talking about hikes today. The ECB raised rates on Thursday. And next week, over four days, four more banks decide. The Bank of Japan on Tuesday, the Fed with a fresh dot plot on Wednesday, the Bank of England and the SNB on Thursday.

## Three things I am watching

First is the yen. USD/JPY is trading near 160, and the market is pricing a Bank of Japan hike to 1.00% with an 80 to 96% probability. That would be the first such rate in Japan since 1995. Since the hike itself is in the price, the message is what makes the move. A hawkish one opens the way toward 157. A dovish one pushes the rate into the 161 to 164 area, and that is where intervention risk starts to rise.

Second is the pound. The Bank of England's rate advantage over the ECB is 175 basis points today, and that is structural support for GBP/USD. Thursday's decision, and the vote split in particular, will show how quickly the market starts pricing the next moves.

Third is the franc, my mood barometer. In risk-off, capital flows into CHF, but with rates at zero the SNB openly states it is ready to intervene against excessive strength. That is why drops in USD/CHF come in steps, not in an avalanche.

## On the calendar today

Preliminary Michigan at 16:00. With CPI at 4.2%, the inflation expectations component alone can move the dollar.

And in the evening, a classic of the trade. Before a week like this, no day-trader's position should sit over the weekend. The Monday gap does not ask anyone's opinion.

## Bench rule for today

The daily range is a budget. Each pair has its typical daily distance, EUR/USD lately around 65 to 70 pips on my broker's data. When the day works through its whole budget and then adds more, statistics stop supporting the chase and start supporting rotation, meaning the move back. Simple, measurable, and it reliably keeps me away from buying tops.

Have a calm Friday and a sensible week.
