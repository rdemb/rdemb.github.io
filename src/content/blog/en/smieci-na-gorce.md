I saw it with my own eyes. People who called themselves experts were buying complete junk at the very top, because FOMO was running at full tilt. The price was rising, so they bought. It kept rising, so they bought more. And then the market did what it always does, and all they had left were charts in red and an excuse that "it is just a correction".

Behind most of those decisions stood someone from Instagram. A guru in a rented car who knows as much about macroeconomics as about flying a plane. He sells emotion, because emotion gets clicks. Interest rates, inflation, valuations, the credit cycle, all of it is too boring for him to turn into a reel.

I prefer something else. Numbers. Statistics. Mathematics. Things that have no mood and need no likes.

## This is not a game

Inflation is a quiet tax that nobody voted for. It eats your savings bit by bit, year after year, and most people only notice it at retirement. Money sitting in an account is not standing still. It loses value, just slowly, so it does not hurt right away.

Without a proper financial cushion and without capital that works, the future is easy to picture. Selling berries at the market to make it to the end of the month. The picture can be completely different. A quiet morning, your own terrace, a fishing rod and fresh fish for lunch, because you took care of the money twenty years earlier.

The car you drive today means little here. What counts is who you will be when you stop working.

## What D-Logic Capital does

I built a private fund where decisions are not made by a hunch or a headline. They are made by a council. A few, sometimes a dozen, of the great minds of investing in one room. Buffett looks at a durable advantage, Graham at the margin of safety, Munger at thinking errors, Taleb at the risk that can blow up a portfolio. Each one judges a company on hard data from the statements, computed from scratch, not on a pretty story.

Then the Red Team steps in and tries to break the thesis before the market does. Only what survives that attack reaches me at full strength.

One iron rule applies. The council advises, and the decision and the money stay in human hands. I count the horizon in years, not in hours. The data is public, the numbers are my own, and every verdict is written down, so that years later you can check who was right. That is what separates this fund from a guru who shouts "buy now" and wipes out the account a month later.
