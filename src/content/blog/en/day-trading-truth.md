When I started, I heard the same thing you are probably hearing: that it is easy. That it does not take much time. That a few indicators and some discipline are enough. I smile now when I think back on it.

The truth is that for the first three years I mostly lost. Steadily. Not spectacularly and not all at once, but bit by bit, month after month. Plus the nerves, and the same question on a loop: why is this not working?

## The trap almost everyone falls into

I looked at the chart and thought in patterns: "we bounced off the moving average, RSI is exhausted, so now it has to go up". Sounds reasonable, right? And that is exactly the trap. It is not analysis, it is telling yourself a story after the fact. The indicator shows the past, and I pretended it showed the future.

The worst part is that this thinking sometimes works. Often enough that you believe you are right. And rarely enough that your account slowly bleeds out.

## Why few people say it

Because "I lost three years and money before I understood anything" does not sell courses. It is easier to show one pretty trade than the hundred you stay quiet about. A good part of this industry lives off the promise that it is fast and simple. It is not.

## What actually changed everything for me

I stopped looking for the magic indicator setting. I started doing something boring: I sat down with historical data and began analysing it really deeply. Not "does this candle look nice", but: what statistically actually happens, when, and why. I started to understand the mechanics of the market, not its decorations.

It was not a eureka moment. It was a slow shift from "I feel it will go up" to "I checked, and here is what the data says". Less emotion, more craft.

## What I would wish for at the start

For someone to tell me plainly: this is hard, long, and sometimes humiliating. Most people lose. An indicator is not an oracle. And the only edge you can build comes from the work nobody wants to do: patient, honest digging through data.

I am not writing this to discourage you. I am writing it to save you part of those three years.
