An OHLC candle is a summary. It tells you where price opened and closed and where it peaked and bottomed, but it loses what happened in between: who was pressing whom, where liquidity disappeared, when a move ran out of fuel. That information lives in the flow of orders, not in the bars.

Order Flow is an attempt to put this in front of your eyes. Four living algorithms, Splot, Rój, Orbita and Nurt, react to the flow in real time. The inspiration was the slime mold Physarum polycephalum, which has no brain yet can solve a maze and find the shortest path. A few simple rules give rise to behavior that looks intelligent. I wanted to see the same thing for order flow.

It is not an entry signal or a finished strategy. It is a way of looking rather than a promise of edge. You can play with it: move the cursor, feed the organism, pull it toward you.

The metaphor holds, because flow really does behave like a living network looking for the path of least resistance. But it is still a visualization of an idea, not proof that I built a real edge on order flow alone. Proof is a separate, far more boring job, and I described it honestly in forex-lab.
