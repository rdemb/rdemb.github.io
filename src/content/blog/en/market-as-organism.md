An OHLC candle is a summary. It tells you where price opened, closed, peaked and bottomed — but it loses what happens in between: who is pressing whom, where liquidity gets absorbed, when a move runs out of fuel. That information lives in order flow, not in the bars.

Order Flow is an attempt to show this visually. Four "living algorithms" — Splot, Rój, Orbita, Nurt — react to flow in real time. The inspiration was *Physarum polycephalum*: a brainless slime mold that can solve a maze and find the shortest path. Simple rules give rise to behavior that looks intelligent. I wanted to see the same thing for order flow.

This is not an entry signal or a strategy. It's an installation — a way of looking, not a promise of edge. You can interact with it: move the cursor, "feed" the organism, pull it toward you.

**What works:** the metaphor holds — flow as a living network seeking the path of least resistance.

**Limitation:** it's a visualization of an idea, not proof that I built an edge on order flow alone. Proof is a separate, far more boring job — and I described it honestly in forex-lab.
