Today I built a company. Not a metaphor for one, but a working skeleton: fourteen AI agents that come up with products, research the market, count the money, design and write code. Each one thinks like someone world-class in their field. The only human in this company is me.

It took one afternoon. That sounds like a brag, so let me say right away where the limit is. But first, here is what it looks like.

## Fourteen people I will never hire

On top is me. Below, five layers and fourteen agents. These are not generic bots in the "you are a helpful assistant" style. Each one got the mind of a specific expert.

Strategy is run by an agent that thinks like Jeff Bezos. The brake is Charlie Munger, the only one on the team allowed to say "this is a stupid idea" and actually stop it. Code is written by someone with the head of DHH, the creator of Rails. The market is researched by an analyst in the style of Ben Thompson. Prices are set by a finance mind like Patrick Campbell. And so on, up to fourteen.

Each has one job and does it well. I call in the ones I need at the moment, usually three or four, not all at once.

## What drives them

Underneath sits a single model: Claude Opus 4.8, on the Max plan from Anthropic. One engine, fourteen roles. The difference between the agents is not different programs, only different ways of thinking that I inject into each as its character, knowledge and rules.

That is the whole trick. I do not need fourteen subscriptions or a server room. I need one good model and a clear division of roles.

## How the first decision looked

To check that this is not just a pretty diagram, I told the team to find a business idea. The analyst combed the market and brought back a dozen opportunities with concrete evidence, not impressions. Munger ran a pre-mortem on each and cut the ones with a hidden flaw. The finance mind counted how many customers it would take to add up. Bezos set the ranking and pointed to the first move.

Out of it came a concrete recommendation, with numbers and an honest "this is what could kill us". I made the decision. The team advises, the human chooses.

## Where the limit is

I will not tell you this is a company that earns money in its sleep. It does not. It is a tool, not magic.

I keep one hard rule. The agents can research, calculate, design and write code on my disk, but nothing goes out without my approval. No payments, no sending emails, no publishing. The human is the gate. That is on purpose, and it is only day one. The rest is not there yet.

I write about it openly, because a result that hides its limitations is an ad, not a report.

## What it is all for

And now the part that genuinely excites me. The potential is enormous, and the only thing that limits us is our imagination.

You do not always need fourteen agents. Sometimes one is enough. Picture an agent that every morning scans freelance listings, picks the ones in your trade, does them from start to finish, and you only check and send. That is already a small business. One person, one agent, recurring income.

Now multiply that by the number of things you do by hand that can be described step by step. That is roughly how much there is to take here.

I did not build a million-dollar company today. I built a machine that helps me find one and stand it up. And this is only the first day on the job.
